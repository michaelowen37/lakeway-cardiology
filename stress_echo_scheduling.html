Stress Echocardiogram:

These can only occur when a cardiologist is in the building and currently, at AUHC, that's Tuesday morning, Thursday or Friday afternoon. Best practice is to visualize Dr. Stoebner's schedule while selecting a time to ensure that the test is not scheduled during his personal days off.

Considering the required days and times above, place an active order into any open slot (on the hour) available on the AUHC Echo schedule (not AUHC EKG).

Important, after selecting a day and time but before selecting "schedule," make sure to change the duration from 30 minutes to 60 minutes.

This avoids the accidental creation of short, unusable slots and reduces risk of double booking patients.

If the patient belongs to Dr. Stoebner, schedule an accompanying follow-up visit so he can review the stress test results with the patient. If a follow-up visit is scheduled on the same day as the actual test, make sure to keep it within the same hour. For example, a 10am stress test would require a 10:40am follow-up with Dr. Stoebner. Note: ECG-read soft blocks can be overridden for this type of follow-up.

Otherwise, follow-ups on any other day do not need to adhere to any specific time restraints.

If the patient does not belong to Dr. Stoebner, no follow-up appointment is necessary.
